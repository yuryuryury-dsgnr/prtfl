01 — Problem & Context

Data is everywhere — but nowhere useful

Engineers across Halliburton spend more time searching for data than actually using it. Critical assets — reports, models, datasets, AI agents — are scattered across disconnected systems with no unified access point, inconsistent metadata, and unclear ownership.

Problem

Fragmented access to critical data

Users cannot efficiently work with data because it is difficult to find, difficult to access, and not easy to compare across multiple sources. There is no single entry point to the organization's data ecosystem.

Goal

One platform for all data assets

Provide a user-friendly means of accessing curated, trusted data, reports, models, and AI Agents — as part of Halliburton's Data Ribbon initiative.

Success looks like

Minutes, not days

Data engineers use the marketplace daily to find, validate, request access, and consume data assets within minutes — not days or weeks.

04 — Research

Understanding users and their journeys

Deep research phase to map out who uses the platform, how they interact with it, and where the friction points are. 4 persona groups, detailed user flows, and end-to-end journey maps shaped every design decision.

Personas — 4 groupsNDA

4 persona groups defined based on interviews and stakeholder workshops, split into Users and Creators & Approvers.

Users

G1

Group 1

Data Scientist / Engineer

Technical specialist working with Python, ML models, and data integrations.

Goals

  • Quickly find relevant datasets and evaluate schema, quality, lineage
  • Get access fast with minimal approvals
  • Ensure data is reliable and stable for production use

Pain Points

  • Hard to find datasets across multiple sources
  • Long access approval cycles
  • Lack of clear owners or documentation
  • Data quality issues (freshness, missing values, drift)
G2

Group 2

Business User

Analysts, product managers, and business stakeholders using BI tools (Power BI, Tableau, Spotfire, Databricks SQL).

Goals

  • Focused on insights rather than discovering raw data
  • Easily find existing reports and dashboards
  • Use officially validated (trusted) datasets and metrics

Pain Points

  • Too many raw technical datasets; too few curated business-ready ones
  • Hard to understand metric definitions
  • Data may be outdated or inconsistent
  • Requires technical support for access or interpretation

Creators & Approvers

G3

Group 3

Data Product Owner

Data product/platform product manager responsible for governance and lifecycle of data products.

Goals

  • Increase adoption of data products and marketplace
  • Ensure data governance, quality, and trust
  • Reduce manual work for data teams

Pain Points

  • Fragmented ownership across teams
  • Too many manual approval steps
  • Data products without proper documentation or quality
  • Slow onboarding of new users or teams
G4

Group 4

Data POD Lead

Team Lead / Tech Lead for a data engineering POD or domain.

Goals

  • Deliver high-quality, reliable data products
  • Ensure data pipelines meet SLA and quality standards
  • Provide correct documentation and metadata

Pain Points

  • Heavy manual work (documentation, access requests)
  • Difficulty ensuring data quality across many pipelines
  • Poor visibility of who uses their data products
  • Managing incidents and schema-breaking changes

05 — Prototype & Design System

From AI brainstorm to production-ready screens

Instead of jumping straight into pixel-perfect mockups, I started with an AI-based prototype to quickly test concepts, layouts, and interaction patterns. This let me brainstorm and validate what works and what doesn't — before investing time in high-fidelity design.

Once the structure was proven, I built a scalable design system with reusable components, tokens, and patterns — then created the final mockups for the dev team.

AI-based prototype

Used AI tools to rapidly generate and iterate on layout concepts, testing different approaches to search, filtering, data cards, and access flows. This phase was about speed and exploration — finding the right direction before committing to a design system.

07 — Reflection

Looking back

What I'd do differently

Involve data governance stakeholders earlier in the process. Their requirements surfaced late and required reworking the access request flow mid-project.

What I learned

Enterprise data products need trust-building at every layer — from metadata quality to access transparency. Users won't adopt a tool if they can't trust the data inside it.
